About Us

The History and Heritage of Illistrin FC

Illistrin FC was established in the mid-1970s, with local figures such as Fr Kieran McAteer laying the foundations for the development and facilitating of youth underage football in the Letterkenny area from his arrival in Letterkenny in 1975. The first formal record of Illistrin's teams competing in amateur football came about when the Letterkenny and District League was established with 6 teams - Illistrin, Lurgybrack, Iona, Glencar, Oldtown and Ard O Donnell, with the first games kicking off in October 1976.

Over the ensuing decades, Illistrin FC grew steadily, entering boys' and girls' teams into local academy and district leagues. Success followed: the U-12 boys, U-14 and U16 teams having won Donegal Schoolboy league and Champions League, while girls' teams claimed U-12, U-14, and U-16 titles in Donegal Women's League competitions.

The club's commitment to the development of youth football saw strong progress in the early noughties with success both on and off the pitch seeing Illistrin FC move to the next level over the preceding years. In the club's earlier years its training and home games were played in the Ballyare pitch(s) and facilities. In 2007, the committee under the stewardship of Chairman Paul Skinnader set out as a key objective to develop a suitable ground and facility for the club. This culminated in the official opening of Illistrin grass and astro pitches in 2012 adjacent to Scoil Naomh Fiacra, Illistrin, Letterkenny.

On the field, in 2013 Seamus Devine's U12 and Paddy Carr's U14 teams both took SFAI Ulster titles, making it to the last 32 in Ireland in the same year. In 2019/20 Dermot McGuiness's U13 squad made it to the last 16 in Ireland in the same competition. In more recent years, Seamus Murphy's U14 squad made it to the Last 16 in the SFAI National cup in 2025.

The club's facilities were renamed Corry Park in August 2022 in memory of Seamus Corry—a lifelong Illistrin player, coach and founding member of the girls' teams—who passed away in 2019.

Today Illistrin FC has over 300 registered players, with over 18 squads competing at U8 - U16 Level in boys and girls blitz age and league ages.
